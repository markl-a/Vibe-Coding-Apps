Apple Inc. was founded by Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne on April 1, 1976, 
in Cupertino, California. The company designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, 
personal computers, tablets, and wearables.

In August 2011, Tim Cook became CEO after Steve Jobs resigned due to health issues. 
Under Cook's leadership, Apple became the first U.S. company to be valued at over $1 trillion 
in August 2018.

Apple operates retail stores in major cities around the world, including New York, London, 
Tokyo, and Shanghai. The company's headquarters, Apple Park, opened in April 2017 in Cupertino.

Some of Apple's most successful products include the iPhone, introduced in 2007, the iPad 
in 2010, and the Apple Watch in 2015. The company also provides services like iCloud, 
Apple Music, and the App Store.
